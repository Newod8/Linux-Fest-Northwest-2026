# Linux-Fest-Northwest-2026
Documentation of my time tabling for I2P at Linux Fest Northwest 2026

### Overview
Over this past weekend, April 24th - 26th, I had the privilege of not only attending but actively contributing to Linux Fest Northwest 2026 in Bellingham, WA. I was invited out by I2P (The Invisible Internet Project) to help table for their company for both days of the event. During the event, I met lots of wonderful and talented people and learned lots of new things about modern Linux-based technologies and research.

### Reflection
At Linux Fest Northwest 2026, companies and organized groups come together to present new Linux-based technologies to hundreds of guests every day. I was able to attend this event representing I2P (The Invisible Internet Project) by coming early to help set up our table on the Friday before the event. By the time the event opened up to the public on Saturday, my group and I were already set up and ready to present ourselves.
![Table Setup Image]()

Every few hours, I would take a break from presenting at our table to walk around and talk to the other companies in attendance. I was able to learn a lot of new things and was even given an old Chromebook to try and jailbreak for free! Throughout the event, I was able to get lots of company merchandise in the form of stickers, t-shirts, and even baseball hats.
![Chromebook Upcycle Image]()

Overall, I had a wonderful time at Linux Fest Northwest and will probably find myself attending similar events in the future. I learned lots of new things, met some incredible people, and went out of my comfort zone to present myself to industry professionals.
